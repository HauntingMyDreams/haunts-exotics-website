General Care

View General Isopod Care

Isopods act as clean-up crews in bioactive setups, breaking down waste and enriching substrate.

  • Humidity: Maintain a moisture gradient
  • Substrate: Organic soil, leaf litter, decaying wood
  • Food: Leaf litter + vegetables + calcium
  • Ventilation: Prevent stagnant air
  • Temperature: 65–75°F

Species Care Guides

Cubaris murina "Florida Orange"

Bright, hardy, and beginner-friendly.

  • Humidity: Moderate to high
  • Behavior: Active breeder
  • Difficulty: Beginner
Cubaris murina "Glacier"

Pale morph with similar care needs.

  • Humidity: Moderate to high
  • Behavior: Active and adaptable
  • Difficulty: Beginner
Armadillidium peraccae

Drier-tolerant species with classic pill behavior.

  • Humidity: Moderate with dry zones
  • Behavior: Rolls into a ball
  • Difficulty: Easy
